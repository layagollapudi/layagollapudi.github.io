Who am I?

I am a current student at the University of Chicago studying Computer Science and Economics. I'm very interested in learning about how technology plays a role in various modern industries. ML / AI Algorithm research, Computer Vision, and Data Systems are fields I would love to get more involved with.

Interested in tech - the start?

My mother, a software engineer and computer scientist, and my father, a computer and electrical engineer, are by far my two largest inspirations for my interest in technology. Seeing my mom as a strong independent woman in tech and my dad get really excited about testing his companys' new products always kept potentially going down such a route a possibility in the back of my mind. My friends in highschool also were planning on expanding their programming knowledge as well, so I decided to try out what they were doing and take a CS class. It was tough, but the reward of creating something after hours of debugging made it all totally worth it.

Interests? Passions?

I've always been interested in helping out others with whatever skills and abilities that I may have. I especially love teaching and working with children, so I have volunteered as an assistant teacher in various settings. I really love being able to learn from those who I am helping as well, and decided to develop this interest by working with students in India to introduce a more well-rounded education system.

Since I was young, I've been singing Carnatic Indian classical music, playing the piano and learning the flute. I started singing when I was 6 years old, and have been performing at local and national events since. As a college, I continue to foster my passion for the Carnatic arts as a member of my school's South Asian Music Ensemble. I learned piano from a classically trained instructor for 6 years, and have been self-taught ever since. I started the flute in 4th grade as a part of our school band and continuted through the band program until the 8th grade, where I switched to joining private ensembles and working on solo performances. My experiences with musical performance also inspired me to start composing music - mainly for the piano - and submitting to various contests.

My musical origins?

I started learning Carnatic music, a form of classical Indian music, when I was about 5 years old. I was very involved from a young age, competing in competitions and performing at various recitals. Around the same time, I also took piano lessons, with which I was not as involved with but still practicing and performing. Later, in elementary school, I started learning how to play the flute as part of my school band, with which I played both solo and group performances throughout my K-12 school years.


How have I come to define music?

I had always loved practicing music as I was instructed by my teachers and gurus, but I also came to realize how much of my musical interests stemmed from the freedom I had as a musician and a performer. I most appreciated the freedom of artistry. I started trying to learn and arrange pieces by ear [thank you very much, perfect pitch] as well as experiment with composition styles and performance textures. I started to play around with and mix different styles of music that I was learning together.
